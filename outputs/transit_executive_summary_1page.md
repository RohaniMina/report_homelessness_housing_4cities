# Executive Summary: Public Transit Recovery After COVID

**Scope:** NYC, SF Bay, Chicago  
**Source:** USDOT/FTA National Transit Database (`8bui-9xvu`)  
**Data Window:** 2002-01 to 2025-12 (baseline for comparison: 2015-2019)

## Bottom Line
- NYC has recovered much more strongly than SF Bay and Chicago.
- By December 2025, NYC is near pre-COVID norms; SF Bay and Chicago remain well below.
- All three systems experienced a severe collapse in April 2020, but recovery trajectories diverged substantially.

## Headline Metrics
- **Latest month (% of 2015-2019 baseline):** NYC **89.9%**, SF Bay **66.7%**, Chicago **62.0%**
- **Trough (April 2020):** NYC **12.5%**, SF Bay **13.4%**, Chicago **18.5%**
- **2025 annual ridership vs 2019:** NYC **86.3%**, SF Bay **69.3%**, Chicago **68.7%**

## What Changed and Why It Matters
- **Recovery gap is now structural:** NYC appears close to a new steady-state near pre-COVID levels; SF Bay/Chicago appear to have settled materially lower.
- **Mode effects are uneven:** Bus/ferry-oriented services recovered faster than commuter-heavy rail in SF Bay and Chicago.
- **Planning implication:** Demand models should assume metro-specific and mode-specific recovery, not a uniform "return to 2019."

## Method Notes
- Metric is **UPT** (unlinked passenger trips), aggregated to metro-level from agency reports.
- Baseline normalization controls for seasonality by comparing each month to the same-month 2015-2019 average.
- "Recent" mode recovery uses the latest 12-month average vs pre-COVID monthly average.
