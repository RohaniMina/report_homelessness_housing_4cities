Transit Ridership Deep Dive (NYC, SF Bay, Chicago)

FTA NTD monthly ridership through 2025-12

TL;DR

  • NYC recovered far more: 89.9% of baseline vs SF Bay 66.7% and Chicago 62.0%.
  • Shared collapse in April 2020: NYC 12.5%, SF Bay 13.4%, Chicago 18.5% of baseline.
  • No metro fully back to 2019 annual total in 2025: NYC 86.3%, SF Bay 69.3%, Chicago 68.7%.

Data and Methodology

  • Agency-reported monthly UPT (unlinked passenger trips), mode-level operational ridership.
  • Source: USDOT/FTA NTD Complete Monthly Ridership dataset (8bui-9xvu).
  • Metro mapping by UZA label for NYC, SF Bay, and Chicago.
  • Baseline: January 2015 to December 2019 (same-month normalization).
  • Key metrics: % of baseline, annual % vs 2019, mode-share shift, and mode-level recovery.

Insights and Visual Findings

1) Recovery paths diverged after a common shock

NYC climbed back near baseline; SF Bay and Chicago stayed materially below pre-COVID levels.

2) Trough-to-latest rebound differs by metro

All metros collapsed in the same month, but the rebound magnitude is strongest in NYC.

3) Annual totals still below 2019

2025 annual ridership remains below 2019 in all three metros, especially SF Bay and Chicago.

4) Mode mix shifted and only partly normalized

Bus/ferry shares expanded during the shock; rail-heavy structures recovered more slowly.

5) Mode-level recovery is uneven

Recent performance varies strongly by mode within each metro, signaling segmented demand recovery.

6) Seasonal strength remains muted

Recent month-by-month levels are below pre-COVID seasonal averages across most months outside NYC.

Detailed Findings

  • Latest recovery (2025-12): NYC 89.9%, SF Bay 66.7%, Chicago 62.0%.
  • Trough month: April 2020 for all three metros.
  • 2025 annual vs 2019: NYC 86.3%, SF Bay 69.3%, Chicago 68.7%.
