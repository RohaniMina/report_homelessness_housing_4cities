Metro Homelessness and Housing Patterns (2011-2026)

TL;DR

  • Homelessness increased across all four cities in HUD PIT data from 2011 to 2024: Chicago (+183.9%), NYC (+174.1%), Los Angeles (+105.7%), San Francisco (+46.8%).
  • The sharpest 2022->2024 increases in NYC and Chicago were mostly sheltered-count growth, while Los Angeles increased in both sheltered and unsheltered counts.
  • Rent pressure rose in all metros (ZORI 2015->2026): Los Angeles +59.2%, Chicago +52.0%, NYC +45.9%, SF +33.1%.
  • Comparable annual PIT for 2026 is not yet available in this run; NYC daily shelter census through 2026-02-18 is +0.14% vs same 2025 YTD window.

Data and Method

  • Nature of data: annual HUD PIT CoC counts, monthly metro rent index (ZORI annualized), and NYC daily operational shelter census.
  • Cities: SF (CA-501), NYC (NY-600), Chicago (IL-510), Los Angeles (CA-600).
  • Method: trend analysis, mayor-term segmentation, 2022->2024 sheltered-vs-unsheltered decomposition, and rent-vs-homelessness comparison.
  • Caveat: PIT methodology differs by year in some CoCs (notably around 2021).

Key Insights and Visualizations

1) Long-run growth is substantial

2) Unsheltered dynamics diverge

3) 2022->2024 composition differs by city

4) Rent pressure is widespread, link is mediated

5) Mayor-period outcomes vary strongly

Interpretation

  • NYC and Chicago recent growth appears concentrated in sheltered systems.
  • Los Angeles remains high with persistent unsheltered burden and only modest recent flattening.
  • San Francisco shows lower net growth than peers and declining unsheltered share after 2019.
  • Housing costs likely raise baseline risk, but policy and capacity channels shape observed counts.
